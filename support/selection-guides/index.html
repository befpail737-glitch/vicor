How to Choose the Right Vicor Module

Step 1: Define Your Requirements

  • Input voltage range
  • Output voltage and current
  • Power level requirements
  • Regulation requirements
  • Isolation requirements
  • Thermal constraints
  • Space limitations
  • Safety standards compliance

Step 2: Determine Module Type

Based on your requirements, determine if you need:

  • DCM: For isolated, fixed-ratio conversion without regulation
  • BCM: For non-isolated current multiplication with voltage transformation
  • PRM: For regulated voltage conversion (often used with VTMs in FPA)
  • VTM: For current multiplication with isolation (used with PRMs in FPA)

Step 3: Consider Packaging Technology

Evaluate which packaging technology best suits your needs:

  • VIA: Vertical Integration Architecture for high power density
  • ChiP: Converter housed in Package for ultimate density
  • Traditional Packages: For applications requiring standard form factors

Step 4: Validate Performance

Use Vicor's tools and simulators to validate your selection, including efficiency calculations, thermal modeling, and system-level simulations.

FAE Perspective: Common Selection Mistakes

Based on our Field Application Engineering experience, here are common mistakes made during module selection:

Overlooking Thermal Considerations

The high efficiency of Vicor modules can sometimes mask thermal design requirements. Always calculate thermal performance under worst-case conditions and ensure adequate heat transfer paths.

Ignoring Transient Requirements

Some Vicor modules have excellent steady-state performance but may require additional filtering or bypassing for applications with high dynamic current demands.

Not Considering System-Level Optimization

Select modules based on overall system performance rather than individual component specifications. The combination of modules (e.g., PRM+VTM) often provides better system-level performance than individual components.

Mismatching Isolation Requirements

Ensure you understand safety and functional isolation requirements. VTMs provide galvanic isolation while BCMS do not, which impacts safety system design.
