Thermal Management in High-Density Power Systems

While Vicor's high-efficiency power modules generate less heat than traditional solutions, thermal management remains critical for long-term reliability and optimal performance. Proper thermal design ensures modules operate within their specified temperature ranges, maximizing both performance and service life.

Our thermal design guidelines provide comprehensive information on effectively managing heat in various applications from data centers to aerospace, considering factors such as ambient temperature, cooling methods, and packaging constraints.

Ambient Temperature Considerations

System thermal design must account for the full operational ambient temperature range, not just room temperature. Many applications require operation in temperatures from -40Â°C to +85Â°C or higher, significantly affecting thermal management requirements.

Power Dissipation Calculations

Power dissipation depends on operating conditions including input voltage, output voltage, load current, and switching frequency:

  • Dissipation = Input Power - Output Power
  • Efficiency impacts dissipation significantly
  • Light-load efficiency also matters for thermal design

FAE Perspective: Thermal Design Practices

Design for Worst-Case Conditions

Effective thermal design must account for worst-case operating conditions:

  • Maximum ambient temperature
  • Maximum power dissipation
  • End-of-life derating
  • System aging effects

For reliability, we recommend designing for normal operating conditions plus appropriate margins, not just maximum ratings.

Module Packaging Impact

Different packaging technologies have different thermal characteristics:

  • VIA packages offer excellent thermal performance through vertical mounting
  • ChiP packages require careful PCB thermal design
  • Traditional packages have standard thermal interfaces
  • Package selection affects thermal design complexity
