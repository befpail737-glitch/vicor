Product Questions

What is the difference between DCM, BCM, PRM, and VTM modules?

DCM (DC-DC Converter Module): Fixed-ratio, isolated converter without regulation. Ideal for bus conversion where downstream converters handle regulation.

BCM (Bus Converter Module): Non-isolated, fixed-ratio converter with current multiplication. Maintains dynamic response of downstream converters.

PRM (Predictable Regulated Bus Converter): Regulated converter designed to work with VTMs in Factorized Power Architecture (FPA).

VTM (Vicor Transformer Module): Current multiplication device with isolation. Works with PRMs in FPA systems.

What is Factorized Power Architecture (FPA)?

Factorized Power Architecture separates voltage regulation from current multiplication using PRM (regulation) and VTM (current multiplication) modules. This approach enables:

  • Optimal efficiency for both functions
  • Enhanced power density
  • Improved thermal management
  • Better dynamic response
What is the difference between ChiP and VIA packaging?

ChiP (Converter housed in Package): Complete power converter in a surface-mount package with minimal external components. Offers ultimate power density.

VIA (Vertical Integration Architecture): Vertical mounting with high pin count for superior thermal performance and high power capability.

How do I determine if I need isolation in my application?

You typically need isolation if you have:

  • Safety requirements (medical, industrial equipment)
  • Ground loop concerns
  • Different ground potentials
  • Protection requirements between power domains

DCMs and VTMs provide galvanic isolation (2250Vdc typical), while BCMs are non-isolated.

What input voltage ranges do Vicor modules support?

Vicor modules support various input voltage ranges depending on the specific model:

  • BCM modules: typically 36-75V, 24-55V, 48-150V
  • DCM modules: typically 24-55V, 36-75V, 48-150V
  • PRM modules: typically 24-55V, 36-75V
  • VTM modules: input range varies with turns ratio

Always check the specific model's datasheet for exact specifications.

Application Questions

How do I choose between DCM and BCM for my 48V system?

Choose a DCM if you need:

  • Galvanic isolation between input and output
  • Fixed-ratio conversion without regulation
  • Isolated power for safety systems

Choose a BCM if you need:

  • Non-isolated current multiplication
  • Preservation of downstream converter dynamic response
  • High efficiency without isolation requirement
Can I parallel Vicor modules for higher power?

Yes, many Vicor modules can be paralleled for higher power applications:

  • DCM modules can be paralleled with proper current sharing design
  • BCM modules have natural current sharing characteristics
  • VTM modules can be paralleled in FPA configurations

Follow application notes for specific paralleling techniques and design guidelines.

How do I implement thermal management for Vicor modules?

Thermal management depends on the module type and application:

  • Ensure adequate PCB copper for heat spreading
  • Use proper thermal vias for vertical heat transfer
  • Consider heat sinks for high-power applications
  • Design for worst-case ambient conditions

Refer to our Thermal Design section for detailed guidelines.

What external components do I need with Vicor modules?

Requirements vary by module type:

  • DCM/BCM/PRM/VTM: Generally require input and output capacitors
  • ChiP modules: Minimal external components due to integration
  • Input filtering: May be required for EMI compliance
  • Output filtering: May be required for ripple requirements

Specific requirements are detailed in each module's application note.

How do I use PRM and VTM together in FPA?

In Factorized Power Architecture:

  1. PRM provides regulated voltage to the input of the VTM
  2. VTM provides current multiplication and voltage transformation
  3. The PRM handles voltage regulation, VTM handles current delivery
  4. This separation allows optimization of each function

This approach provides high efficiency and power density for demanding applications.

Technical Questions

How efficient are Vicor modules?

Efficiency varies by module type and operating conditions:

  • DCM modules: Up to 96% efficiency
  • BCM modules: Up to 97% efficiency
  • PRM modules: Up to 95% efficiency
  • VTM modules: Up to 98% efficiency

Efficiency remains high across wide load ranges due to ZVS technology.

How do Vicor modules handle EMI/EMC requirements?

Vicor modules have excellent EMI performance due to ZVS technology and optimized design:

  • Zero Voltage Switching reduces high-frequency switching noise
  • Complementary power flow minimizes ripple current
  • ChiP packaging reduces EMI through enclosed design
  • May require additional input/output filtering for compliance

Refer to our Application Notes for EMI design guidance.

What protection features do Vicor modules have?

Protection features vary by module type but typically include:

  • Over-temperature protection
  • Input over-voltage protection
  • Output over-current protection
  • Short circuit protection

Specific protection features are detailed in each module's datasheet.

How do Vicor modules handle load transients?

Dynamic response characteristics vary by module type:

  • BCM modules: Preserve downstream converter response for fast transients
  • DCM modules: Fast response since no regulation loop
  • PRM modules: Regulated response with defined bandwidth
  • VTM modules: Fast response due to current multiplication nature

System-level response depends on the load characteristics and output capacitance.

What is the typical lifetime of Vicor modules?

Vicor modules are designed for long-term reliability:

  • Mean Time To Failure (MTTF) typically exceeds 100,000 hours
  • Designed for 20+ year operational life in typical applications
  • Components selected for long-term reliability
  • Derating applied to ensure robust operation

Reliability data is available in the product datasheets and reliability reports.
