Advanced Packaging for High-Density Power

Vicor's advanced packaging technologies are fundamental to achieving the industry's highest power density in power conversion. Our proprietary packaging solutions including ChiP (Converter housed in Package) and VIA (Vertical Integration Architecture) enable exceptional power density while maintaining excellent thermal and electrical performance.

These packaging technologies address the critical challenges of space-constrained applications while providing the performance and reliability required for demanding power conversion applications across various industries.

ChiP (Converter housed in Package) Technology

Technology Overview

ChiP technology represents a breakthrough in power conversion packaging, housing an entire power converter within a surface-mount package. This eliminates external components like magnetics, capacitors, and resistors, resulting in the industry's highest power density solution.

Key Advantages

  • Ultimate Power Density: Highest power density available in a surface-mount package
  • Component Integration: All components enclosed within a single package
  • Manufacturing Simplicity: Single placement instead of multiple components
  • EMI Performance: Superior EMI characteristics due to enclosed design
  • Thermal Performance: Optimized thermal design for efficient heat transfer

Applications

ChiP technology is ideal for applications with extreme space constraints or where maximum power density is required:

  • Telecom and networking equipment
  • Data center servers and switches
  • Automotive electronics
  • Portable and wearable devices

Design Considerations

When implementing ChiP solutions, consider:

  • PCB thermal design for heat extraction
  • Package placement for thermal performance
  • System-level EMI requirements
  • Handling and assembly processes

VIA (Vertical Integration Architecture) Technology

Technology Overview

VIA technology features vertical mounting of the converter with high pin count connections to the PCB, providing excellent thermal performance and high power capability in a compact solution. The vertical orientation and multiple connections provide superior thermal and electrical performance.

Key Advantages

  • Superior Thermal Performance: Multiple thermal paths to PCB
  • High Power Capability: Suitable for high-power applications
  • Current Handling: Excellent high-current performance
  • EMI Performance: Optimized for low EMI designs
  • Reliability: Robust mechanical and electrical connections

Applications

VIA technology is ideal for applications requiring high power and excellent thermal performance:

  • High-power computing systems
  • Industrial power systems
  • Telecom power supplies
  • Test and measurement equipment

Design Considerations

When implementing VIA solutions, consider:

  • PCB pad design for mechanical support
  • Enclosure height constraints
  • Assembly and rework processes
  • Underfill or encapsulation requirements

FAE Perspective: Packaging Selection Guidelines

Selection Criteria

When selecting packaging technology, we consider multiple factors:

  • Power Density Requirements: ChiP for ultimate density, VIA for high power
  • Thermal Constraints: VIA for superior thermal performance
  • Manufacturing Considerations: ChiP simplifies assembly
  • Application Requirements: Environmental, vibration, etc.

Common Selection Mistakes

Common packaging selection mistakes include:

  • Choosing based solely on power density without considering thermal requirements
  • Underestimating the impact of packaging on PCB thermal design
  • Not considering manufacturing or assembly constraints
  • Overlooking EMI implications of different packaging technologies

Hybrid Approaches

Sometimes a hybrid approach provides optimal results, using different packaging technologies in the same system:

  • Using VIA for high-power primary conversion
  • Using ChiP for space-constrained PoL conversion
  • Combining technologies for optimized system performance
