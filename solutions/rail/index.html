Rail Transportation Power Solutions

Reliable, high-reliability power solutions designed specifically for the demanding requirements of railway and mass transit systems. Our power modules meet stringent rail standards for safety, reliability, and performance in harsh environmental conditions.

Power Solutions for Critical Rail Applications

Railway and mass transit systems demand power solutions that meet the most stringent requirements for safety, reliability, and performance. Power systems in rail applications must operate continuously in harsh environments while meeting strict safety standards and electromagnetic compatibility requirements.

Our rail-qualified power solutions are engineered to provide decades of reliable operation in demanding applications including locomotive traction systems, signaling and communication systems, passenger comfort systems, and safety-critical applications. All solutions are designed to meet applicable rail standards including EN 50155 and EN 45545.

Standard Application Description Power System Requirements

EN 50121 EMC for Railway Applications Electromagnetic compatibility for railway equipment EMI emissions and immunity requirements
IEC 61373 Shock & Vibration Railway applications: Rolling stock equipment Shock and vibration testing for equipment durability
EN 50124 Insulation Coordination Insulation coordination for railway applications Creepage, clearance, and insulation requirements
EN 50125 Environmental Conditions Environmental conditions for railway applications Temperature, humidity, atmospheric pressure requirements

Environmental & Safety Considerations

Vibration and Shock Tolerance

Railway equipment experiences significant vibration and shock during normal operation:

  • Continuous vibration in rail environment
  • Shock loads during switching and coupling
  • Track irregularities causing mechanical stress
  • Wind loads on wayside equipment
  • Verified with IEC 61373 testing

Design Guidelines:

  • Robust mechanical mounting
  • Elimination of stress concentrations
  • Proper connector selection
  • Secure component placement
  • Flexible interconnects where needed

Temperature & Humidity

Rail equipment must operate across wide temperature range and varying humidity:

  • Operating temperatures: -40°C to +85°C
  • Storage temperatures: -55°C to +125°C
  • Humidity: 5% to 95% RH (non-condensing)
  • Thermal cycling durability
  • Condensation protection for outdoor equipment

Protection Methods:

  • Wide temperature design margins
  • Thermal management design
  • Enclosures with proper sealing
  • Heaters for condensation prevention
  • Conformal coating for moisture protection

Electrical Environment

Rail systems present challenging electrical environments:

  • Wide voltage variations on train lines
  • High EMI from traction systems
  • Lightning and surge exposure
  • Ground potential differences
  • RF interference from communication systems

Electrical Protection:

  • Wide input voltage range modules
  • High EMI immunity
  • Comprehensive surge protection
  • Isolated power systems
  • Robust filtering

Safety Requirements

Rail systems must meet stringent safety requirements:

  • Safety Integrity Levels (SIL) requirements
  • Fail-safe design principles
  • Functional safety compliance
  • Redundancy requirements
  • Safe state implementation

Safety Implementation:

  • Redundant power systems
  • Diversity in power solutions
  • Safe output shutdown capability
  • Diagnostic coverage
  • Failure mode analysis
