Overview of Railway Vehicle Power Requirements

Railway applications present unique power challenges: systems must operate reliably in harsh environmental conditions, withstand significant vibration and shock, and meet strict safety and electromagnetic compatibility standards. Power systems must accommodate the wide voltage ranges typical of railway power systems and operate continuously for extended periods without maintenance.

Our railway power solutions leverage Vicor's high-efficiency power conversion technologies specifically designed for rail applications. These solutions meet international rail standards including EN 50155 for rolling stock electronic equipment and EN 45545 for fire protection requirements. With high efficiency and robust design, these solutions ensure reliable operation in demanding rail environments.

Engineering Perspective: Railway Power Systems

Key Design Considerations

When implementing power solutions in railway vehicles, consider these critical factors:

Rail Standards Compliance: All power solutions must meet railway-specific standards including EN 50155 (Electronic equipment used on railway vehicles), EN 50121 (EMC requirements), and EN 45545 (Fire protection).

Environmental Robustness: Railway power systems must operate in extreme temperature ranges (-40Â°C to +85Â°C), withstand high vibration and shock levels, and resist dust, moisture, and corrosive environments.

System Reliability: High reliability and long MTBF are critical for railway applications where maintenance access may be limited. Redundancy and failure mode analysis are essential design considerations.
