Overview of Tethered Drone Power Requirements

Tethered drones present unique power challenges: they must operate continuously for days or weeks while carrying minimal payload weight. Power systems must achieve exceptional efficiency to minimize losses in the power transmission from ground to drone through the tether. Additionally, the power solution must operate in the harsh environmental conditions typical of outdoor deployments.

Our tethered drone power solutions leverage Vicor's high-efficiency power conversion technologies to maximize the power delivered to the drone's systems while minimizing power loss in the tether and onboard conversion. With efficiency exceeding 95%, these solutions enable longer flight times and more capable payloads.

Engineering Perspective: Tethered Drone Power Optimization

Key Design Considerations

When implementing power solutions in tethered drone systems, consider these critical factors:

Power Transmission Efficiency: The tether resistance creates a significant power loss proportional to the square of the current (IÂ²R losses). Higher voltage power transmission (e.g., 48V instead of 12V) significantly reduces these losses, which is why our solutions emphasize high-efficiency conversion.

Thermal Management: Tethered drones have limited opportunities for heat dissipation. Our high-efficiency power modules reduce heat generation, and we provide thermal design guidance for optimal performance in constrained spaces.

Altitude Operation: Reduced air pressure at altitude affects thermal performance and may impact the operation of switching power supplies. Our components are selected and tested to operate reliably under these conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is 48V preferred over 12V for tethered drones?

At the same power level, 48V systems have 1/16th the IÂ²R losses of 12V systems. This dramatically reduces power losses in the tether, allowing more power for the drone's systems.

How do you address the weight constraint of drone platforms?

Our power modules use high-frequency switching and advanced magnetics to achieve high power density. Additionally, higher efficiency reduces waste heat, allowing for lighter thermal management.

Can the power systems handle the vibration and motion of flight?

Yes, our power modules are designed for harsh environments with high vibration and shock tolerance, making them suitable for drone applications.

How does the system handle voltage variations in the tether?

Our power modules feature wide input voltage ranges and excellent line regulation to maintain stable output voltage despite tether voltage variations.
