Automotive Power Solutions

Advanced power conversion solutions for automotive applications including 48V mild hybrid systems, electric vehicle power conversion, and high-performance electrification systems. Our AEC-Q100 qualified power modules meet the rigorous requirements of automotive environments.

Powering the Future of Mobility

The automotive industry is experiencing an unprecedented transformation with electrification, advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS), and the move toward autonomous vehicles. Modern vehicles now require sophisticated power systems to support everything from 48V mild-hybrid systems to high-power electric drivetrains.

Our automotive power solutions deliver the efficiency, power density, and reliability required for these demanding applications. All solutions are designed to meet automotive industry standards including AEC-Q100 qualification where applicable, ensuring long-term reliability in harsh automotive environments.

Automotive Power Architectures

48V to 12V Conversion Architecture

In mild-hybrid systems, a 48V electrical system enables power recovery during braking and provides power for higher-current loads. Efficient 48V to 12V conversion is critical for:

  • Maximizing fuel efficiency gains
  • Ensuring compatibility with 12V systems
  • Providing power for high-demand accessories

Typical Implementation:

  • BCM modules for galvanic isolation and current multiplication
  • High-efficiency conversion with >96% efficiency
  • EMI filtering for automotive noise requirements

Electric Vehicle Power Architecture

Electric vehicles require sophisticated power conversion for multiple voltage domains:

  • High-voltage battery to 400V/800V for traction inverters
  • High-voltage to 48V for high-power auxiliary systems
  • High-voltage to 12V for traditional vehicle loads

Key Considerations:

  • High efficiency to extend range
  • Galvanic isolation for safety
  • Compact size for space-constrained packaging

ADAS Power Distribution

For ADAS and autonomous vehicle systems, power distribution must maintain signal integrity while providing fast transient response:

  • Low-noise power for sensitive sensors
  • Fast response for AI processing units
  • High reliability for safety-critical systems
  • EMI control for RF-sensitive radar/LiDAR systems
  • Power tree design to minimize noise coupling

Solution Approach:

  • Point-of-load regulation near loads
  • Low-noise power modules
  • Appropriate filtering for each subsystem
  • Isolation between sensitive systems

Automotive Design Considerations

Environmental Requirements

Automotive power systems must operate reliably in harsh environments:

  • Temperature range: -40°C to +105°C (with derating for higher temperatures)
  • Vibration and shock resistance per automotive standards
  • Humidity and corrosion resistance
  • EMI/EMC compliance for vehicle systems
  • Chemical resistance to automotive fluids

Power Quality Requirements

Power system design must meet automotive-specific requirements:

  • Load dump protection (up to 60V) for 12V systems
  • Reverse voltage protection for installation errors
  • Start-stop voltage variations
  • Fast transient response for dynamic loads
  • Low EMI to avoid interference with RF systems

Efficiency & Thermal Management

Efficiency directly impacts fuel economy and range:

  • 96%+ efficiency needed for meaningful fuel savings
  • Minimized thermal dissipation in engine bay
  • Effective heat dissipation for continuous operation
  • Thermal protection and derating capability
  • Conduction cooling in space-constrained applications

Reliability & Service Life

Automotive systems must operate reliably for vehicle life:

  • 15+ year service life expectation
  • MTBF calculations and prediction reports
  • 100% testing of production lots
  • Lot traceability for field reliability
  • Obsolescence management and support
