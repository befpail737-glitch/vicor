Overview of Automotive 48V/12V Requirements

The automotive industry is transitioning to 48V electrical systems to support advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS), electric power steering, active suspension, and other high-power loads. This requires reliable, high-efficiency DC-DC conversion between the 48V domain for high-power loads and the traditional 12V domain for conventional automotive systems.

Our 48V/12V conversion solutions address the unique challenges of automotive applications: stringent safety standards, harsh environmental conditions, high reliability requirements, and limited space and weight constraints. With AEC-Q100 qualified components and automotive-grade design practices, these solutions ensure safe and reliable operation.

Key Design Considerations

When implementing 48V/12V conversion in automotive applications, consider these critical factors:

Automotive Standards: All components must meet automotive qualification standards including AEC-Q100 for integrated circuits and ISO 26262 for functional safety. The system must also meet EMI/EMC standards for automotive environments.

Environmental Requirements: Automotive power systems must operate reliably in extreme temperature ranges (-40Â°C to +105Â°C for underhood applications) and withstand high vibration, shock, and humidity conditions.

Efficiency & Thermal: High efficiency is critical to minimize losses and heat generation, which is essential for the confined and thermally constrained automotive environment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is 48V being adopted in modern vehicles?

48V systems can deliver more power with lower current, reducing conductor losses and enabling new high-power automotive features like active suspension, electric turbochargers, and advanced ADAS systems.

How does 48V/12V conversion impact vehicle efficiency?

High-efficiency conversion minimizes power losses between voltage domains. Our solutions achieve over 95% efficiency, helping to maximize vehicle range.

What automotive standards do your solutions meet?

Our solutions use AEC-Q100 qualified components and are designed to meet automotive EMI/EMC standards and ISO 26262 functional safety requirements.

Can these solutions handle automotive electrical transients?

Yes, our solutions include protection for common automotive electrical transients including load dump, reverse battery, and cold crank conditions.
