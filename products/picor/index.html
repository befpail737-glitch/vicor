Overview of Vicor Picor Products

Picor, a product line from Vicor, features advanced power management ICs that complement Vicor's modular power solutions. The Cool-Power series includes ZVS (Zero Voltage Switching) regulators that provide high efficiency in compact point-of-load applications. These semiconductor solutions leverage Vicor's expertise in power conversion to deliver optimized performance in demanding applications.

Picor products are engineered to address the challenges of high-efficiency, high-density power conversion at the point-of-load. The ZVS topology significantly reduces switching losses, enabling high-frequency operation while maintaining excellent efficiency across wide load ranges.

FAE Perspective: Cool-Power ZVS Regulators

Cool-Power ZVS regulators excel in applications where efficiency at light loads is critical. The ZVS technology significantly reduces switching losses, making them ideal for battery-powered systems and applications with wide load variations. The high switching frequency allows for small external components, reducing overall solution size. Consider Cool-Power when you need high efficiency in a compact PoL solution with good light-load performance.

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I use Cool-Power ZVS vs traditional PWM regulators?

Use Cool-Power ZVS regulators when you need superior light-load efficiency and high switching frequencies in space-constrained applications.

What are the thermal considerations for Cool-Power ICs?

Due to ZVS operation, these ICs generate less switching loss heat, but proper thermal management is still essential for maximum reliability.

Can Cool-Power regulators be paralleled for higher current?

Yes, many Cool-Power regulators support paralleling for higher current applications using current sharing techniques.

FAE Perspective: Power System Controllers

Power System Controllers are essential in complex power systems requiring precise sequencing, voltage monitoring, and system-level protection. These controllers enable sophisticated power architectures that would be difficult to implement with discrete components. The digital interfaces allow for system-level monitoring and control, providing valuable insights into power system performance. Consider these controllers when your application requires multiple power rails with coordinated startup and protection.
