DCM (DC-DC Converter Module) series features high-efficiency, fixed-ratio bus converters that provide isolated power conversion. These modules utilize Vicor's ZVS technology to achieve up to 96% efficiency in a compact form factor.

DCM Modules Overview

DCM modules represent Vicor's implementation of high-efficiency, fixed-ratio bus converters with galvanic isolation. These devices are designed for applications where you need efficient DC-DC conversion without regulation. The fixed-ratio transformation (typically 1:1 or 2:1) makes them ideal for unregulated bus conversion applications.

The ZVS topology significantly reduces switching losses, making DCMs particularly effective in high-power, high-density applications. The isolated design provides safety isolation and noise immunity while enabling bidirectional power flow.

Key Features & Benefits

High Efficiency

Up to 96% efficiency achieved through ZVS technology reduces power losses and thermal management requirements.

Galvanic Isolation

2250Vdc isolation barrier provides safety isolation and prevents ground loops in distributed power systems.

Fixed Ratio Conversion

Achieves 1:1, 2:1, or other fixed ratios depending on model, with tight voltage tracking between input and output.

Compact Packaging

Available in ChiP, VIA, and traditional packages for space-constrained applications without sacrificing performance.

FAE Perspective: Optimizing DCM Performance

When implementing DCMs in your design, consider the following for optimal performance:

DCM modules excel in applications where you need a simple, efficient DC-DC conversion without regulation. Their fixed-ratio nature makes them ideal for bus conversion applications. The ZVS technology significantly reduces switching losses, making them particularly effective in high-power, high-density applications. Consider DCM when regulation is handled downstream by other converters.

For thermal management, ensure proper mounting and heatsinking as DCMs generate heat despite high efficiency. The thermal resistance from junction to case is critical for long-term reliability. Pay attention to input and output capacitor placement to minimize conducted noise and ensure stable operation.

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I use a DCM vs. a regulated converter?

Use DCM modules when you need efficient DC-DC conversion without regulation. They're ideal for bus conversion where downstream converters handle regulation.

What are the thermal management considerations for DCM modules?

DCM modules generate less heat due to high efficiency, but proper thermal management is still critical. Use appropriate heat sinking and consider the thermal resistance from junction to case.

Can DCM modules be paralleled for higher power?

Yes, DCM modules can be paralleled for higher power applications, but ensure proper current sharing through careful design of the power distribution network.

What is the dynamic response of DCM modules?

DCM modules have very fast dynamic response due to the lack of regulation loop. They respond immediately to load changes, but downstream regulation is required for precise voltage control.
