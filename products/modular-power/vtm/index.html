VTM (Vicor Transformer Module) modules are current multiplication devices that provide voltage transformation and isolation. These modules work with PRM modules in Factorized Power Architecture (FPA) to deliver exceptional power density and efficiency.

VTM Modules Overview

VTM modules represent Vicor's implementation of current multiplication devices with voltage transformation and galvanic isolation. VTMs perform current multiplication with voltage transformation, making them ideal for applications requiring high output current at lower voltage. When paired with PRMs in an FPA configuration, they enable remarkable power density by separating voltage regulation from current delivery.

The high efficiency and isolation make VTMs suitable for safety-critical applications. VTMs can be used independently, but their full benefits are realized when used with PRMs in Factorized Power Architecture configurations.

Factorized Power Architecture with VTMs

In Factorized Power Architecture (FPA), VTMs work in conjunction with PRMs to separate voltage regulation from current multiplication:

  • PRMs provide regulated voltage to the input of VTMs
  • VTMs provide current multiplication while maintaining voltage transformation
  • The galvanic isolation provides safety and noise immunity
  • This separation enables optimization of both functions independently

The FPA approach enables superior power density and efficiency compared to traditional power architectures, especially in applications requiring both tight voltage regulation and high current delivery.

FAE Perspective: Optimizing VTM Performance

VTMs perform current multiplication with voltage transformation, making them ideal for applications requiring high output current at lower voltage. When paired with PRMs in an FPA configuration, they enable remarkable power density by separating voltage regulation from current delivery. The high efficiency and isolation make them suitable for safety-critical applications.

VTMs can be used independently, but their full benefits are realized when used with PRMs in Factorized Power Architecture configurations. The fixed turns ratio determines the voltage transformation and inversely affects current multiplication (e.g., a 4:1 voltage ratio results in 4:1 current multiplication).

For thermal management, VTMs benefit from the low switching losses due to their ZVS operation. The VIA packaging offers excellent thermal performance, but proper PCB thermal design is still important for high-power implementations. Consider the system-level impact of isolation when designing the power distribution network.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do VTMs provide current multiplication?

VTMs use a fixed turns ratio to transform voltage while inversely transforming current, effectively multiplying current while stepping down voltage.

What are the advantages of using VTM modules?

VTMs provide high current density, galvanic isolation, and exceptional efficiency, especially when combined with PRMs in FPA systems.

Can VTMs be used without PRM modules?

VTMs can be used independently, but their full benefits are realized when used with PRMs in Factorized Power Architecture configurations.

What is the difference between VTMs and BCMs?

VTMs provide galvanic isolation, while BCMs are non-isolated. VTMs are designed for FPA systems with PRMs, while BCMs are standalone solutions.
