Vicor VTM™High Power Series - Technical Details & Specifications

Modular Power / VTM • January 1, 2025 • Technical Article

The VTM High Power Series features high-efficiency Vicor Transformer Modules designed for Factorized Power Architecture (FPA) systems. These modules provide current multiplication with galvanic isolation when used with PRMs (Primary Regulated Modules), delivering high current at low voltage from an intermediate bus voltage with exceptional efficiency and power density.

Overview

The VTM High Power Series represents Vicor's solution for current multiplication with galvanic isolation in Factorized Power Architecture (FPA) systems. VTMs (Vicor Transformer Modules) work in conjunction with PRMs (Primary Regulated Modules) to create a complete power solution. The VTM provides current multiplication and isolation, while the PRM provides regulation and power factor correction.

This architecture allows for optimization of each function independently, resulting in a power system with superior efficiency, power density, and dynamic response. VTMs are fixed-ratio devices operating at high frequency, which enables high power density and efficiency.

Key Features

Current Multiplication

VTM modules multiply current while reducing voltage based on the turns ratio, ideal for low-voltage, high-current applications.

Galvanic Isolation

Provides safety isolation and noise immunity with high voltage standoff between input and output.

High Efficiency

Achieves up to 98.2% efficiency through fixed-ratio transformation at high switching frequency with minimal losses.

Fast Transient Response

Preserves power delivery dynamics for excellent load transient response when paired with appropriate PRM.

Design Considerations

Factorized Power Architecture

VTMs must be used with PRMs to form a complete power solution. The PRM provides regulation to the intermediate bus voltage, while the VTM provides current multiplication and isolation to the load. The output voltage of the system is determined by both the PRM regulation and the VTM turns ratio.

Layout Recommendations

For optimal performance in FPA systems, ensure proper decoupling between PRM and VTM stages. Minimize loop inductance between the PRM and VTM to maintain efficiency and stability. Provide adequate thermal management for both PRM and VTM in the system design.

System Integration

VTM High Power modules excel in applications where you need isolated, high-current power delivery with preserved dynamics. They are ideal for systems where load transients are handled by the upstream PRM stage.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do VTMs differ from traditional transformers?

VTMs are active transformer modules that operate at high frequency with minimal losses, achieving very high efficiency and power density. They are designed specifically for FPA systems and provide current multiplication with galvanic isolation.

What is the relationship between PRM and VTM in FPA?

The PRM provides regulation to an intermediate bus voltage (typically 48V), while the VTM provides current multiplication and isolation to the load. The output voltage is determined by the PRM regulation and the VTM turns ratio.

Can VTMs be used without PRMs?

VTMs are designed for use with PRMs in FPA systems to create a complete regulated power solution. However, they can be used with other regulated sources if the input voltage and operating conditions are appropriate.
