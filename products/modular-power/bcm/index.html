BCMâ„¢ Modules (Bus Converter Modules)

BCM (Bus Converter Module) modules are non-isolated, fixed-ratio converters that provide high-current, high-efficiency power conversion. These modules are designed for applications requiring current multiplication with minimal voltage transformation.

BCM Modules Overview

BCM modules represent Vicor's implementation of high-efficiency, non-isolated bus converters designed specifically for current multiplication applications. Unlike traditional DC-DC converters, BCMs provide current multiplication with voltage transformation while maintaining the dynamic response characteristics of downstream regulators.

The non-isolated design of BCMs contributes to the extremely high efficiency, while the VIA package provides excellent thermal performance. The fixed-ratio transformation maintains the dynamic response of the downstream converter, which is beneficial in fast-transient applications.

Key Features & Benefits

Current Multiplication

BCM modules provide current multiplication capabilities, ideal for applications requiring high output current at lower voltage.

Non-Isolated Design

Non-isolated design contributes to extremely high efficiency while maintaining compact form factor.

Dynamic Response Preservation

Maintains the fast transient response of downstream converters without regulation delay.

VIA Packaging

Vertical Mount Technology provides excellent thermal performance and compact solution size.

FAE Perspective: Optimizing BCM Performance

BCM modules are exceptional for applications needing current multiplication, such as 48V to 12V conversion in data center applications. Their non-isolated design contributes to the extremely high efficiency, while the VIA package provides excellent thermal performance. The fixed-ratio transformation maintains the dynamic response of downstream converters, which is beneficial in fast-transient applications.

The non-isolated nature means input and output share a common ground. Ensure your system design accounts for this when implementing multiple power domains. BCM modules can be paralleled for higher current applications with natural current sharing due to their inherent characteristics.

For optimal performance, ensure proper input and output filtering. The BCM's high-frequency operation may require additional filtering components compared to traditional converters. The VIA packaging offers excellent thermal performance but requires careful PCB layout for optimal heat transfer.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes BCM modules different from other converters?

BCM modules are unique in their fixed-ratio, non-isolated design with current multiplication capability. They maintain the dynamic response of downstream converters.

How do I handle the non-isolated nature of BCM modules?

Non-isolated means input and output share a common ground. Ensure your system design accounts for this when implementing multiple power domains.

Can BCM modules be used in parallel?

Yes, BCM modules can be paralleled for higher current applications, with natural current sharing due to their inherent characteristics.

What is the difference between BCM and VTM modules?

BCM is non-isolated with current multiplication, while VTMs provide isolation. VTMs work with PRMs in Factorized Power Architecture, while BCMS are standalone solutions.
