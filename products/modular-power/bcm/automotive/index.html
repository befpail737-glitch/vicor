The BCM Automotive Series features AEC-Q100 qualified current multiplication bus converters with bidirectional power flow designed specifically for automotive applications. These modules are engineered for the harsh automotive environment while maintaining Vicor's signature high efficiency and power density, providing high-current solutions for automotive power systems.

Overview

The BCM Automotive Series represents Vicor's solution for automotive applications requiring current multiplication with preserved dynamics. These devices are specifically designed and tested to meet automotive industry requirements for reliability, environmental conditions, and electromagnetic compatibility.

The fixed-ratio transformation (typically 1:4, 1:2, or 1:1) makes them ideal for applications where you need high current at a lower voltage from a higher voltage source in automotive systems. The bidirectional design enables power flow in both directions, suitable for regenerative braking and other automotive applications.

Key Features

AEC-Q100 Qualified

Full AEC-Q100 qualification ensures reliability and performance in harsh automotive environments with extreme temperature and vibration.

Current Multiplication

BCM modules multiply current while reducing voltage based on the turns ratio, ideal for high-current automotive applications.

Bidirectional Power Flow

Supports power flow in both directions, essential for regenerative braking and battery charging/discharging in electric vehicles.

Automotive-Grade Reliability

Engineered and tested to meet automotive industry standards for vibration, shock, temperature range, and electromagnetic compatibility.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does AEC-Q100 qualification mean?

AEC-Q100 is an industry-standard qualification for integrated circuits in automotive applications. It includes tests for temperature, humidity, vibration, and electromagnetic compatibility.

How do automotive BCMs differ from standard BCMs?

Automotive BCMs are AEC-Q100 qualified, have extended temperature range qualification, and additional testing for automotive-specific environmental conditions and EMC requirements.

What automotive standards do these modules meet?

BCM Automotive modules are designed to meet automotive industry standards for EMI, vibration, shock, temperature cycling, and other environmental requirements.
