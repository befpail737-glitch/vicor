Vicor Input Filter Solutions

Advanced EMI filtering solutions for power systems. Our M-FIAM and QPI series provide superior EMI performance while maintaining compact form factors and high reliability in demanding applications.

Input Filter Technologies Overview

In today's complex power systems, electromagnetic interference (EMI) poses significant challenges to system performance and regulatory compliance. Vicor's input filter solutions provide superior EMI suppression while maintaining high efficiency and compact form factors.

Our filter technologies address both common-mode and differential-mode noise across a wide frequency spectrum, ensuring your power systems meet stringent EMI regulations while maintaining high power density and efficiency.

Understanding EMI in Power Systems

Common-Mode vs. Differential-Mode Noise

Common-Mode (CM) Noise: Appears equally on both power lines with respect to earth ground. Generated by parasitic capacitances in transformers and semiconductor switching.

Differential-Mode (DM) Noise: Appears between the power lines. Generated by the switching action of power converters and conducted through the power path.

Effective EMI filtering requires addressing both CM and DM noise components.

Frequency Considerations

EMI requirements vary by frequency range:

  • 9kHz - 150kHz: Conducted emissions (mostly differential mode)
  • 150kHz - 30MHz: Both common and differential mode emissions
  • 30MHz - 300MHz: Radiated emissions (often caused by common mode currents)

Vicor filters are designed to provide attenuation across these critical frequency ranges.
