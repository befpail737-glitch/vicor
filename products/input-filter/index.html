As an authorized Vicor distributor, we provide genuine Input Filter solutions including M-FIAM and QPI series. These high-performance EMI filters ensure clean power delivery and compliance with electromagnetic compatibility standards in demanding applications.

Overview of Vicor Input Filter Products

Vicor's Input Filter portfolio addresses critical power integrity challenges in high-performance power systems. Our M-FIAM (Filter in Array Module) and QPI (Quad Pack Isolated) series provide exceptional EMI filtering while maintaining compact form factors. These filters are essential components in power systems that require compliance with electromagnetic compatibility standards and protection against conducted noise.

These input filters incorporate advanced magnetic and capacitive elements to suppress both common-mode and differential-mode noise. The modular construction and integrated packaging reduce assembly time and improve system reliability.

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I use M-FIAM vs discrete filtering?

Choose M-FIAM when you need predictable, tested EMI performance in a compact solution. Discrete filtering offers more flexibility but requires more design work and validation.

What are the thermal management considerations for M-FIAM filters?

M-FIAM filters have low DC resistance to minimize heating, but ensure adequate airflow and consider thermal derating at high ambient temperatures.

Can M-FIAM filters be paralleled for higher current?

Yes, M-FIAM filters can be paralleled for higher current applications, but ensure matched components and current sharing.

FAE Perspective: QPI Filters

QPI filters are ideal for isolated power applications where you need EMI filtering on multiple isolated rails. The four-channel design allows for filtering of isolated DC-DC converter inputs with a single component. The galvanic isolation provides enhanced safety and noise immunity for safety-critical applications. Consider QPI when you need both isolation and EMI filtering in a compact solution.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes QPI filters different from standard EMI filters?

QPI filters provide galvanic isolation in addition to EMI filtering, making them suitable for isolated power supply applications.

How do I handle the isolated nature of QPI filters?

The isolated nature means input and output grounds are separated. Ensure your system design accounts for this isolation requirement.

Can QPI filters be used with non-isolated converters?

While possible, QPI filters are specifically designed for isolated power applications where the isolation feature provides additional value.
